12 Tips For Disaster Prevention


Tips

1. Stay informed - Tune in to local authorities for information about evacuations and safety tips.
2. Have a plan for evacuation - Know where you will go during a natural disaster and how you will get there.
3. Keep emergency kits on hand - Stock kits with flashlights, batteries, first aid supplies, and important identification information.
4. Avoid unnecessary risks - Do not leave your home unless instructed to do so.
5. Go to the safest area in your home - During a flood, go to a higher floor. If a tornado is in the area, go to a basement or inner room on the bottom floor of your home.
6. Get home insurance - If your house gets damaged, insurance can cover the cost.
7. Get health insurance - Insurance will pay for your treatment if you are injured.
8. People - First, move people and pets or other animals to safety.
9. Prescriptions - Make sure you take any necessary prescriptions, including hearing aids, eyeglasses, or asthma medication.
10. Papers - Grab any important documents, including identification information.
11. Personal needs - Take items you might need such as food, water, clothing, or emergency supplies.
12. Priceless items - Bring valuables, photos, mementos, heirlooms, or anything that is irreplaceable.
